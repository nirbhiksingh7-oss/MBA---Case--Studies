# Nucor Corporation in 2018 — Strategic & Financial Analysis

**Type:** MBA case study  ·  **Frameworks:** Porter's Five Forces, SWOT

A strategic examination of the lowest-cost U.S. steel producer navigating foreign
competition, underperforming assets, and growth decisions — paired with a five-year
financial performance review.

## The question
How should Nucor use its 2018 position — strong finances, Section 232 tariff
protection, and the broadest product portfolio in North America — to extend its
competitive advantage?

## Approach
- **Industry analysis** via Porter's Five Forces (notably foreign dumping — Chinese
  subsidies drove 36% import share in 2017).
- **SWOT and distinctive-competency** assessment (low-cost electric-arc-furnace model,
  50+ years of profitability, performance culture).
- **Financial performance review, 2013–2017:** net sales **$20.3B**, net earnings
  **$1.3B**, net margin **6.5%**, **ROE 17.2%** — recovery to the strongest condition
  since 2008.
- Identified **six strategic issues** and developed **six management recommendations**.

## Key recommendations
- Use the tariff window to **pay down debt** and build cash reserves.
- **Accelerate the shift** from commodity steel to higher-margin value-added products.
- **Target automotive OEMs** with new galvanizing and cold-mill capacity.
- **Resolve the $750M Louisiana DRI** investment via rigorous ROI analysis.
- **Restructure the European JV** to redeploy ~$413M to higher-return opportunities.
- **Formalize the leadership pipeline** to protect culture during acquisition-led growth.

## Key takeaway
Strong financials are an opportunity, not a conclusion. The analysis connects industry
forces and financial performance to specific capital-allocation moves — what to fund,
what to fix, and what to exit.
