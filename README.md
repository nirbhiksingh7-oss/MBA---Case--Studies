## 1. East Kalimantan Mine Acquisition — Investment Analysis
**Tools:** Excel (financial modeling) · 📄 [View the analysis](East_Kalimantan_Mine_Investment_Analysis.pdf)

**The question:** Should Diamond Energy acquire a coal mine — 12M tons reserves,
~1M tons/year production, $30M CapEx?

**Approach**
- Built free cash flows (2015–2026) from after-tax operating profit, depreciation, CapEx, and working capital.
- Derived **WACC of 16.09%** via CAPM (beta 1.4) and a 40/60 debt-equity weighting.
- Evaluated **NPV ≈ +$1.2M**, **IRR ≈ 17.6%**, **payback ≈ 6 years**, **profitability index ≈ 1.04**.
- Ran capital-structure sensitivity and a three-scenario risk analysis (base / –25% / +20% price).
- Assessed financing & bankability — debt service coverage, covenants, hedging, parent guarantees.

**Recommendation:** Conditional approval — the base case is marginally positive but highly
sensitive to coal price and financing. Approve only with a parent equity guarantee, tighter
covenants, and price hedging / cost controls.

## 2. Nucor Corporation in 2018 — Strategic & Financial Analysis
**Frameworks:** Porter's Five Forces, SWOT · 📄 [View the analysis](Nucor_2018_Strategic_Analysis.pdf)

**The question:** How should Nucor — the lowest-cost U.S. steel producer — use its 2018
position (strong finances, tariff protection, broadest product portfolio) to extend its
competitive advantage?

**Approach**
- Industry analysis via Porter's Five Forces (foreign dumping drove 36% import share in 2017).
- SWOT and distinctive-competency assessment.
- Financial review 2013–2017: net sales **$20.3B**, net earnings **$1.3B**, net margin **6.5%**, **ROE 17.2%**.
- Identified six strategic issues and developed six management recommendations.

**Key recommendations:** Use the tariff window to pay down debt; shift toward value-added
products; target automotive OEMs; resolve the $750M Louisiana DRI via ROI analysis;
restructure the European JV to redeploy ~$413M; formalize the leadership pipeline.
